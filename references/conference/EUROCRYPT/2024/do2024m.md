## 笔记

Blind signatures allow the issuing of signatures on messages chosen by the user so that they ensure blindness of the message against the signer. Moreover, a malicious user cannot output ℓ+1 signatures while only finishing ℓ signing sessions. This notion, called one-more unforgeability, comes in two flavors supporting either sequential or concurrent sessions. In this paper, we investigate the security of a class of blind signatures constructed from Sigma-protocols with small challenge space $\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}$ (i.e., polynomial in the security parameter), using $k$ repetitions of the protocol to decrease the chances of a cheating prover. This class of schemes includes, among others, the Schnorr blind signature scheme with bit challenges and the recently proposed isogeny-based scheme CSI-Otter (Crypto'23), as well as potential blind signatures designed from assumptions with the well-known Sigma-protocol for the graph-isomorphism problem (e.g., Lattice Isomorphism Problem). For this class of blind signatures, we show a polynomial-time attack that breaks one-more unforgeability for any ℓ ≥ k concurrent sessions in time $O(k \cdot |\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}|)$. Contrary to the ROS attack, ours is generic and does not require any particular algebraic structure. We also propose a computational trade-off, where, for any $t \leq k$, our attack works for $\ell = \frac{k}{t}$ in time $O(\frac{k}{t} \cdot |\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}|^t)$. The consequences of our attack are as follows. Schemes in the investigated class of blind signatures should not be used concurrently without applying specific transformations to boost the security to support more signing sessions. Moreover, for the parameters proposed for CSI-Otter ($k=128$ and $|\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}|=2$), the scheme becomes forgeable after 128 concurrent signing sessions for the basic attack and with only eight sessions in our optimized attack. We also show that for those parameters, it is even possible to compute two signatures in around 10 minutes with just one signing session using the computation power of the Bitcoin network. Thus, we show that, for sequential security, the parameter $k$ must be at least doubled in the security parameter for any of the investigated schemes.

盲签名允许对用户选择的消息签发签名，使签名者无法获知消息内容（即消息盲性）。此外，恶意用户在完成 ℓ 次签名会话后，不应能输出 ℓ+1 个有效签名。这一特性称为"多一不可伪造性"（one-more unforgeability），分为支持顺序会话和并发会话两种形式。本文研究一类由 Sigma 协议构造的盲签名方案的安全性，这类方案的挑战空间 $\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}$ 较小（即安全参数的多项式级别），并通过将协议重复 $k$ 次来降低作弊证明者成功的概率。该类方案包括使用比特挑战的 Schnorr 盲签名、近期提出的基于同源的方案 CSI-Otter（Crypto'23），以及基于图同构问题等经典 Sigma 协议所对应困难假设（如格同构问题）设计的潜在盲签名方案。

对于该类盲签名，我们展示了一种**多项式时间攻击**：对于任意 ℓ ≥ k 的并发会话，该攻击可在时间 $O(k \cdot |\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}|)$ 内破坏多一不可伪造性。与 ROS 攻击不同，我们的攻击是通用的，无需依赖任何特定的代数结构。我们还提出了一种计算权衡：对于任意 $t \leq k$，攻击可针对 $\ell = k/t$ 个会话，在时间 $O(\frac{k}{t} \cdot |\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}|^t)$ 内完成。

攻击的影响如下：该类盲签名方案在并发场景下不应直接使用，必须通过特定变换提升安全性以支持更多签名会话。对于 CSI-Otter 的建议参数（$k=128$，$|\mathcal{C}_{\Sigma}|=2$），在基础攻击下经过 128 次并发会话后方案即可被伪造，在优化攻击下仅需 8 次会话。此外，我们还表明对于这些参数，利用比特币网络的计算能力，仅凭一次签名会话即可在约 10 分钟内计算出两个签名。因此，对于顺序安全性，本文研究的所有方案中参数 $k$ 至少需要翻倍（相对于安全参数）。
