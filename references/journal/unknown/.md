## 笔记

We introduce Spartan, a new family of zero-knowledge succinct non-interactive arguments of knowledge (zkSNARKs) for NP, where the prover's work scales with the size of the NP statement's witness. A distinctive feature of Spartan is that it is transparent: it does not require a trusted setup. Spartan's core is a new, efficient interactive proof protocol for an NP-complete problem of multilinear extensions and a novel commitment scheme for multilinear polynomials. We prove security of Spartan in the random oracle model under the discrete log hardness assumption. Among schemes with a transparent setup, Spartan offers the best prover performance.
